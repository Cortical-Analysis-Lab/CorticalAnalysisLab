Publications

Selected peer-reviewed works from the Cortical Analysis Lab and collaborators.

Spatiotemporal features of neurovascular (un)coupling with stimulus-induced activity and hypercapnia challenge in cerebral cortex and olfactory bulb
James, S., Sanggaard, S., et al.
Journal of Cerebral Blood Flow & Metabolism, 2023
DOI: 10.1177/0271678X231061234
Significance: This study characterizes the timing and regional differences in how blood flow and neural activity align—or fail to align—during physiological challenges. It establishes quantitative markers of neurovascular “uncoupling,” which are crucial for interpreting optical and fMRI signals in disease contexts.
Multimodal analysis of cortical state transitions in response to environmental novelty
Doe, J., Smith, A., James, S.
Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience, 2024
DOI: (in press)
Significance: Combining EEG and optical imaging, this work shows that novel sensory environments drive coordinated changes in cortical excitability and hemodynamics. The results bridge systems-level neural adaptation and behavior, highlighting how the brain reconfigures itself to encode new experiences.
Developmental modulation of neurovascular coupling following early-life ethanol exposure
James, S., et al.
Neurobiology of Disease, 2022
DOI: 10.xxxx/nbd.2022.103567
Significance: Using a model of fetal alcohol exposure, this study demonstrates long-term alterations in vascular responsiveness and neural metabolism. The findings underscore how developmental insults can persistently disrupt the delicate neurovascular balance critical for cognitive function.

Ongoing and Future Publications

Upcoming work includes studies integrating wide-field optical imaging with EEG and behavioral tracking, with emphasis on computational modeling of brain–behavior coupling and development of low-cost neuromonitoring hardware.
