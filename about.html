Biography

Dr. Shaun James is a neuroscientist at Sacred Heart University whose work integrates multimodal brain imaging, electrophysiology, behavioral analysis, and computational modeling to investigate the fundamental and translational mechanisms underlying cortical function.

His research centers on the coordination of neural, glial, and vascular signals during behavior and under altered physiological states. Using synchronized wide-field optical imaging, EEG, and machine-learningâ€“enhanced behavioral tracking, Dr. James develops frameworks to understand how brain states emerge from dynamic biological networks.

He also leads interdisciplinary initiatives involving neuroengineering, experimental neuroscience, and student-driven research innovation. Dr. James mentors undergraduate and graduate researchers in imaging, artificial intelligence, experimental design, and quantitative neuroscience.

His broader interests include translational models of neurodevelopmental conditions, neurovascular plasticity, cortical dysfunction, neural signal decoding, and the design of multimodal tools for next-generation neuroscience research.
