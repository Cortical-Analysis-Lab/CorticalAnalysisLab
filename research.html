Research

Exploring how neural, vascular, and metabolic systems interact to support brain function and behavior.

Neurovascular and Neurometabolic Coupling

The brain’s activity depends on tightly coordinated relationships between neurons, glia, and blood vessels. Our lab studies how changes in blood flow and metabolism reflect — or sometimes fail to reflect — the underlying neural signals. We use optical imaging, EEG, and behavioral metrics to measure these relationships in vivo.

Why This Matters

Disrupted neurovascular coupling is a hallmark of numerous disorders, including stroke, FASD, and neurodegeneration. Understanding these interactions provides insight into both normal and pathological brain function.

Multimodal Integration

Our experiments combine multiple data streams — widefield fluorescence, hemodynamic reflectance, and electrophysiological recordings — to map how cortical states evolve over time. These multimodal datasets allow us to connect large-scale network dynamics with specific behavioral and physiological contexts.

Developmental and Translational Models

Using rodent models, we investigate how early-life environmental factors alter the coordination of neural and vascular systems. Our long-term goal is to identify biomarkers that bridge basic research and clinical application.

Future Directions

The lab is actively developing new hardware and analysis pipelines to link optical signals with EEG and behavioral data in real time — paving the way for translational neurotechnology applications.
